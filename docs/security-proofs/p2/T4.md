## Proof Strategy

Direct reduction from a double-opening adversary to a short-kernel-vector finder.
The argument is the standard linear-commitment binding reduction used for SIS- and
module-SIS-based commitments: subtract the two accepted openings, obtain a nonzero
short vector in the kernel of the public commitment map, and contradict the cited
hardness assumption.

Therefore, under those cited assumptions and the two implementation conditions
above, the accumulator commitment is binding.

## Assumptions Required

1. **RingSIS/M-SIS hardness at the frozen parameters.**
2. **Linear commitment form.** The accumulator commitment must be a linear lattice
   commitment `Com_A(w) = A Â· w mod q`, not merely an opaque hash surrogate.
3. **Arithmetic norm enforcement.** `validate_witness` must reject any witness with
   `\|w\|_\infty > 17`, so the difference of two accepted openings is guaranteed to
   satisfy the shortness bound needed by the reduction.
4. **Consistent encoding.** Accepted openings must be interpreted in the same
   ambient module/ring so that subtraction of valid openings is well-defined.

## What Is Not Yet Implemented

This proof is **conditional**, not unconditional, because two required items are
still open in the current prototype:

1. **Arithmetic norm enforcement in `validate_witness`.**
   `crates/pvthfhe-aggregator/src/folding/mod.rs` does not yet enforce the
   coefficient bound needed by the reduction; the current check is only a harness
   predicate on `proof_bytes` shape.
2. **Replacement of the SHA-256 hash-chain surrogate with a linear lattice commitment.**
   The current accumulator uses a SHA-256-based surrogate rather than the linear
   algebraic commitment required to turn a double opening into a RingSIS/M-SIS
   kernel vector.

Until both items are implemented, the theorem should be read as documenting the
exact conditions under which accumulator binding follows, rather than as an
unconditional statement about the present code.
